# A Quiet Morning in the City  

It was one of those mornings where the light felt soft and deliberate, not harsh like the usual noon sun. The streets were still half‑empty, and the air carried a faint smell of coffee and fresh bread.

Everything moved slower, and for once it felt okay not to rush.

## The Light Through the Windows  

As the sun rose higher, the light began to cut through the windows of old brick buildings. The narrow alleys turned into long lines of gold and shadow, and every parked bike, every window frame, every cracked wall suddenly had a role in the scene.

You could hear distant traffic, but it felt like background music rather than an interruption.

## A Cup of Coffee and a Pause  

In one small café by the corner, an elderly man sat alone with a small espresso and a notebook. He wasn’t in a hurry. He simply watched the street, writing something down, then looking up again.

That moment reminded me that sometimes the best stories don’t start with a grand plan—they start with a cup of coffee and a quiet decision to pay attention.
